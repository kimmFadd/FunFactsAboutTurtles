WHAT IS TURTLE?

Turtles are reptiles of the order Testudines, characterized by a special shell developed mainly from their ribs. Modern turtles are divided into two major groups, the Pleurodira (side necked turtles) and Cryptodira (hidden necked turtles), which differ in the way the head retracts. There are 360 living and recently extinct species of turtles, including land-dwelling tortoises and freshwater terrapins. They are found on most continents, some islands and, in the case of sea turtles, much of the ocean. Like other amniotes (reptiles, birds, and mammals) they breathe air and do not lay eggs underwater, although many species live in or around water.

Turtle shells are made mostly of bone; the upper part is the domed carapace, while the underside is the flatter plastron or belly-plate. Its outer surface is covered in scales made of keratin, the material of hair, horns, and claws. The carapace bones develop from ribs that grow sideways and develop into broad flat plates that join up to cover the body. Turtles are ectotherms or "cold-blooded", meaning that their internal temperature varies with their direct environment. They are generally opportunistic omnivores and mainly feed on plants and animals with limited movements. Many turtles migrate short distances seasonally. Sea turtles are the only reptiles that migrate long distances to lay their eggs on a favored beach.

Turtles have appeared in myths and folktales around the world. Some terrestrial and freshwater species are widely kept as pets. Turtles have been hunted for their meat, for use in traditional medicine, and for their shells. Sea turtles are often killed accidentally as bycatch in fishing nets. Turtle habitats around the world are being destroyed. As a result of these pressures, many species are extinct or threatened with extinction.


Turtles, like other reptiles, lay eggs that are slightly soft and leathery. The eggs of the biggest species are round, while the eggs of other species are stretched. Sea turtles lays their eggs on dry, sandy beaches. Turtles often take several years to mature and start breeding. In many cases, they reproduce every few years instead of annually.


Turtles have a tough beak that they use to cut and chew their food. Instead of teeth, their upper and lower jaws are covered by hard ridges. Carnivorous turtles have sharp ridges for cutting through prey, while herbivorous turtles have serrated ridges to help them eat tough plants. Turtles use their tongues to swallow food, but unlike many other reptiles, they can't stick out their tongues to catch food.


The biggest turtle in the world is the great leatherback sea turtle, reaching a shell length of 200 cm (80 inches) and weighing over 900 kg (2,000 lb, or 1 short ton). Freshwater turtles are usually smaller, but the largest species, the Asian softshell turtle Pelochelys cantorii, has been reported to measure up to 200 cm or 80 inches. .


Scientists recently found that a turtle's organs don't wear out or become less effective as the turtle gets older, unlike many other animals. They observed that the liver, lungs, and kidneys of a hundred-year-old turtle are nearly the same as those of a young turtle. This discovery has motivated genetic researchers to explore the turtle's genes to understand why they live so long.
